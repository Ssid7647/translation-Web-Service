Web GIS enabled Road information Management & Monitoring System (GRIMMS)
PMGSY (Pradhan Mantri Gram Sadak Yojana - Prime Minister's Rural Road Programme) was launched by the Govt. of India to provide road connectivity to unconnected rural habitations as part of a poverty reduction scheme.
GIS enabled Road Information Management & Monitoring System (GRIMMS) to provide GIS interface to the Online Monitoring and Management System (OMMS) and manage various activities under 'Pradhan Mantri Gram Sadak Yojana (PMGSY)'.
Salient Features:
Efficient and User friendly G-Governance application
The system uses data from heterogeneous databases.
It has been designed to have a live link with external database (OMMS, developed and implemented before the GIS project was conceived).
Data updates happen through the MIS system (OMMS), and is reflected in GRIMMS Web dynamically.
Dynamic map generation and query based on external MIS database
Easily accessible & Easy availability of timely and up-to-date information
GIS and MIS databases updated and managed independently.
Cost Effectiveness
Efficient management and monitoring of rural roads
Project monitoring in space and time
Decision making through objective analysis
Guiding investment decisions to match habitation with appropriate roads
Right to information - Correct and authentic information readily available
Information about government plan regarding construction/updating of roads
Provides tourist places information- a possible extension of the product for tourism
Easy tracking of work progress
More Transparency in system
Public access
States Covered - Rajasthan, Himachal Pradesh, Odisha, Mizoram, Tripura, Manipur.
