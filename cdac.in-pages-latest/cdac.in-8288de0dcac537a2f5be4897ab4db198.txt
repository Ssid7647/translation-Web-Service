MoU signed for devp of Sindhi language
Sakal Times
October 19, 2015
The National Council for Promotion of Sindhi language, an autonomous body under the Union Ministry of Human Resource Development, Department of Higher Education and the GIST Group, Centre for Development of Advanced Computing (C-DAC), Pune signed a joint Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) on October 13.
The MoU, which is of two yearsâ€™ duration, covers a wide range of subjects and deliverables with the prime objective of the development and propagation of Sindhi language on the digital medium, of which the main deliverables are dictionaries, converters from Arabi Sindhi to Devanagari and vice-versa to bridge the digital divide and above all means and measures in the shape of a road-map to ensure that Sindhi script and language find their rightful place on the digital medium, such as workshops and training programs.
Ravi Teckchandani, Director NCPSL, signed the MOU on behalf of NCPSL.
The signing ceremony of the MoU was attended by Aruna Jethwani, Vice-Chairperson NCPSL and senior members of the Council as well as Language experts of Sindhi.
Mahesh Kulkarni, Associate Director and HoD, GIST and his team represented C-DAC.
